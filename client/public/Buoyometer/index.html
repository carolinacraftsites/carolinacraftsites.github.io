The Buoyometer

An accessible body composition tool using hydrostatic weighing — no lab required.

What Is the Buoyometer?

The Buoyometer is a device that allows a person to self-measure hydrostatic weight. It is used in a pool or other body of liquid of known density to counter the force of gravity pulling the body downwards, giving you a direct reading of body composition.

What Is Hydrostatic Weighing?

Hydrostatic weighing, also known as underwater weighing or hydrodensitometry, is a technique for measuring the density of a living person's body.

It is a direct application of Archimedes' principle — that an object displaces its own volume of water. A person with more bone and muscle will weigh more in water, meaning they have a higher body density and lower percentage of body fat.

Why Measure Body Fat Percentage?

Body fat percentage (BFP) is the total mass of fat divided by total body mass, multiplied by 100. Body fat includes essential body fat and storage body fat. Essential body fat is necessary to maintain life and reproductive functions. Understanding your body composition helps track fitness progress far more accurately than weight alone.

How Does the Buoyometer Work?

  1. In a relatively shallow part of a pool, submerge and sit legs crossed on the bottom.
  2. Fully exhale all the air from your lungs.
  3. Slowly pull down the Buoyometer, keeping it vertical in the water.
  4. When you feel your body lift off the bottom, look at the Buoyometer and make sure it's still vertical.
  5. Seal the "gauge tube" with your thumb to mark the level of submersion.
  6. Re-surface and read the result via the gauge tube. This is the amount of air (float) necessary to make your body equal to the density of the water.

What Does the Gauge Indicate?

The gauge on the Buoyometer indicates your body fat percentage — a simple, readable measurement you can take yourself in any pool.

Who Would Use the Buoyometer?

  • Athletes
  • Medical Professionals
  • Weight Lifters

Depending on what your doctor recommends, you will be better able to understand how your body composition impacts certain key areas of your health and well being.
